Edinburgh Line Up

the Liquid Room - November 20, 2013
9c Victoria Street, Edinburgh EH1 2HE

Rachel Sermanni

In the last 2 years 21 year old Highlander and folk noir balladeer, Rachel Sermanni, has played over 400 shows across Asia, North America and numerous headlining tours of the UK and Europe; a No 1 single in the Independent Charts; iTunes Single of the Week with Waltz resulting in 6 0,000+ downloads; the release of her debut album ‘Under Mountains’ to critical acclaim. Rachel has spent a large part of 2013 touring North America including the Dawson City Music Festival and Interstellar Rodeo Festivals where she shared main stages with the likes of Steve Earl, Sharon Van Etten, The Alabama Shakes and Vampire Weekend.

Vigo Thieves

Vigo Thieves formed in 2008 and are from Wishaw 20-miles outside of Glasgow. They combine to produce epic songs that are fit for stadiums and festivals. There are echoes of band favourites like New Order, Simple Minds and U2; driven beats, explosive synthesisers and soaring emotionally charged vocals with lyrics that can be sung from the heart and soul.

Bwani Junction

Scottish indie pop band, Bwani Junction, have been causing a stir over the summer. Now they’re ready to unleash their infectious, anthemic Borneo on October 28th. The band has previously released Civil War and Papa Candy, which still spin frequently on XFM, Absolute Radio and Radio 1. They also have a special fan in Huw Stephens: with a recorded session on his show and constant support for the band, he also tipped Bwani Junction in NME, “They’re from Edinburgh, they’re all brilliant musicians, they have an inspired take on guitar pop and they are a joy to see onstage.”
